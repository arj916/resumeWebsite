Science Exhibit

The challenge of this project was to create an activity that would be fun, educational, and intuitive for elementary school kids. My team and I addressed this challenge by incorporating 3D printed building blocks so the kids have a hands-on activity. Bulletproofing the code so that the kids would still be able to complete the entire activity despite pressing the wrong button or providing the wrong input. Including pre and post activity quizzes to ensure the kids retain the new concepts from the exhibit. This resulted in a streamline design that where the elementary schoolers had the chance to build towers with different building materials(blocks) and see if they would withstand different weather conditions. A post activity survey showed that 94.6% of kids said the exhibit was fun. Also the results of the pre, and post quizzes show a 47.1% increase in correct answers after completing the activities.

Pasta Bridge

My team and I were tasked with constructing a bridge that can hold the largest amount of weight possible. The restrictions for the bridge include that it is made out of only pasta, and adhesive, that weights less 125grams, spans a chasm that is 30 cm long, and should have space for a 4cm "person" to cross the bridge. During this process, my team had to overcome balancing stability with weight, determining which materials to use and making a bridge that balanced, with no obvious weak section. The end result included 2 designs that were made out of fettuccini, lasagna, and hot glue. We incorporated triangles and cross sections to promote stability with minimal weight increased. The testing of the final products showed that design 1 could hold 23.7 times its own weight and design 2 could hold 22.1 times its own weight.

Action Figure

I was tasked with creating a unique laser cut figure that could stand up by itself, and had 5 or more custom parts. The main challenges of the project were the material being very weak, the conversion from a 2-D design to a 3-D final product, and an unknown margin of error from laser cutter. I overcame these challenges by adjusting my measurements by a small margin and using the moldable characteristics of cardboard to my advantage to promote stability.
